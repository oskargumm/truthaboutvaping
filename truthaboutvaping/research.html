Young People’s Use of E-Cigarettes across the United Kingdom: Findings from Five Surveys 2015–2017

Study shows that even if young people try e-cigarettes they are not likely to become regular users.

E-cigarettes emit very high formaldehyde levels only in conditions that are aversive to users: A replication study under verified realistic use conditions

Study shows that fomaldehyde levels in e-cigarette vapor that was previously tested were not done under realistic conditions. And are in fact much lower.

Are Metals Emitted from Electronic Cigarettes a Reason for Health Concern? A Risk-Assessment Analysis of Currently Available Literature

Study shows that levels of metals emitted by e-cigarettes are of no signifcant health concern for vapers.

Evaluation of Electronic Cigarette Liquids and Aerosol for the Presence of Selected Inhalation Toxins

Study compares diacetyl and acetyl propionyl in e-cigarettes and cigarettes. Shows that smokers get ~100 times more of diacetyl then vapers do. Still not good for anyone.
